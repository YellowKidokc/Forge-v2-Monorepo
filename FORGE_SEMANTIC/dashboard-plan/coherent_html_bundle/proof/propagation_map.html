The Propagation Principle

Whatever you choose at Q2 becomes your substrate. That substrate must answer every subsequent question — Q3 through Q12 — without contradiction. Where it can't answer, that's a blank. Where it contradicts itself, that's a death. Where it answers only by borrowing from another substrate's vocabulary, that's strain. The accumulated cost of "strain" positions becomes unsustainable — typically by Q6–Q8.

At each node, published secular theories are embedded — showing what physics already proved and what questions it left front and center. Those open questions converge on the same shape: the form of the boundary conditions (BC1–BC8).
